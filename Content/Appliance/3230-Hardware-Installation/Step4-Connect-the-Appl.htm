Step 4: Provide Power to the Appliance

The Solace PubSub+ 3230 has two redundant AC hot‑swappable power supplies (PS1 and PS2) in bays at the rear of the appliance.
A single installed power supply meets the appliance requirements. The second installed power supply provides redundancy, load sharing, and increased appliance availability, and can be removed (or inserted) without affecting appliance operation.
The total power required by the Solace PubSub+ 3230 under full message load is 450 W. This power is shared among the number of power supplies installed.

This procedure is intended for a qualified power supply installer who is experienced with electronic circuitry and wiring practices. Use the following information in conjunction with the safety information contained herein, and the safety guidelines listed in Preparing Your Site.

If only one power supply is connected to the appliance when the appliance powers up, the appliance incorrectly reports that it has redundant (not simplex) power. To correct this condition at anytime, temporarily connect to the appliance power from a second power supply, then remove it.

  • To prevent equipment damage from electrostatic discharge, always ensure that the appliance chassis is electrically connected to earth ground. Always follow ESD prevention procedures when removing and replacing power supplies. Use an antistatic wrist strap, or another antistatic device. If no wrist strap or mat is available, ground yourself by touching the metal part of the chassis.
  • If one of the redundant power supplies in your appliance is found to be defective, Solace recommends that you leave the defective power supply in the appliance until the replacement unit arrives to ensure that the appliance maintains the correct airflow. Refer to Replacing Power Supplies for the replacement procedure.

To turn on power for the Solace PubSub+ 3230:

  1. Verify that an appropriate AC power source is operational and turned on.

    AC-powered appliances rely on the building’s installation or power supply for overcurrent protection. Ensure that a listed and certified fuse or circuit breaker is used to protect the appliance from short circuits.

  2. Connect the provided AC power cables to the power connectors located on each AC input power supply.

    The Solace PubSub+ 3230 with an AC-input power supply uses a three‑pronged International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) connector for an AC power cord.

  3. Connect the AC power cords into a three-terminal, single-phase power source that provides AC power within the acceptable range.
  4. Use a paper clip to press the () on/standby button on the front panel of the appliance, located on the right-hand side below the LEDs, to turn power on to the appliance.
  5. Monitor the LEDs on the front of the appliance to verify that it is powering up properly.

    Refer to Connections, LEDs, and Specifications for LED status explanation.

  6. Verify that the Green LED located in the upper right-hand corner of each power supply is on

    The CLI login prompt displays on your management console screen:

    SolOS release x.x.x.x

    login:

    The appliance can now be quickly configured through the setup Privileged EXEC command in the Solace CLI. Refer to Initializing Appliances for details.

Troubleshooting

If you encounter problems when you power on the appliance, check the following items:

  • With the power on, is the blue PWR LED on the front panel on?
    • If not, check the AC input, AC source, appliance circuit breaker, and the AC power supply cable.
    • If the blue PWR LED is still off, the problem might be a power supply failure. Verify that the Green LED located in the upper right-hand corner of each power supply is on.
  • Does the appliance shut down after being on for a short time?
    • Check the fans. If the fans are not working, the appliance overheats and shuts itself down.
    • Ensure that the chassis intake and exhaust vents are clear.
