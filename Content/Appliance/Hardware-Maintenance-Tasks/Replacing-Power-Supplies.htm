Power Supply Replacement

This section provides information for how to replace power supplies in Solace PubSub+ appliances.

If your appliance is running with redundant power supplies installed and one is found to be defective, Solace recommends that you leave the defective power supply in the appliance until the replacement unit arrives to ensure that correct airflow is maintained in the appliance.

ESD/Safety Precautions

Before hot swapping appliance power supplies, review all safety warnings in this section.

Use this information in conjunction with the safety information contained herein and the safety guidelines listed in Preparing Your Site.

To avoid the risk of personal injury or death from electric shock, before performing any of the following power supply procedures, unplug the power cord from the power source outlet on AC units to ensure that power is removed.


Wear protective gloves when hot swapping the power supply to keep from being burned. Under full load conditions the power supply can be very hot.


To prevent equipment damage from Electrostatic Discharge (ESD), always ensure that the appliance chassis is electrically connected to earth ground. Always follow ESD prevention procedures when removing and replacing power supplies. Use an anti-static wrist strap, or another anti-static device. If no wrist strap or mat is available, ground yourself by touching the metal part of the chassis.

Tool and Equipment Requirements

  • No. 1 Phillips head screwdriver (PubSub+ 3230/3260s only)
  • protective gloves
  • electrostatic discharge wrist strap
  • anti-static mat
  • replacement power supply (supplied by Solace)

Replacing Solace PubSub+ 3230/3260 Power Supplies

PubSub+ 3230s and PubSub+ 3260s house multiple hot‑swappable AC power supplies in bays at the rear of the unit. Hot swapping allows you to remove and install a power supply without powering down the appliance.

A PubSub+ 3230 is provided with two power supplies. A single installed power supply meets the PubSub+ 3230 requirements, while the other installed power supply provides redundancy, load sharing, and increased event broker availability. The second power supply can be removed without directly affecting event broker operation.

A PubSub+ 3260 with a CHS-3260AC-02, CHS-3260AC-03, or CHS-3260AC-04 chassis is provided with four power supplies. Two installed power supplies meet the PubSub+ 3260 requirements; the other installed power supplies provide redundancy, load sharing, and increased event broker availability. They can be removed without directly affecting event broker operation.

Step 1: Removing Power Supplies

To remove a power supply, use the following procedure:

  1. Use an anti-static wrist strap or other device to ground yourself.
  2. Put on protective gloves to keep from being burned by a hot power supply.
  3. Facing the rear of the appliance, identify the failed power supply by checking the bay that it is installed in, and checking that the Green LED on the power supply is off.

    Power Supply Rear View

  4. Unplug the power cable from the power connector to ensure that power is removed.
  5. Loosen by hand the captive screw located above the AC-input power connector.
  6. Grasp the metal handle on the power supply and firmly pull to slide the failed power supply out of the bay while holding the release located at the top right of the power supply (as shown below).

    Removing Power Supply from Chassis (Solace PubSub+ 3260 Shown)

  7. Return the failed power supply to Solace.
  8. Install a replacement power supply as described in Installing Power Supplies.

Step 2: Installing Power Supplies

To install a power supply, use the following procedure:

  1. Allow the power supplies to reach room temperature before installing.
  2. Use an anti-static wrist strap or other device to ground yourself.
  3. Do the following for each power supply to be installed, consecutively, one at a time:
    1. Inspect the power supply to ensure that the pins and electrical connections are not damaged.
    2. Turn the latch thumbscrew counterclockwise to ensure that the latching mechanism is free. When properly loosened, the thumbscrew remains in the latching mechanism but is able to move freely.

      Loosened Latch Screw—Top-view of Power Supply Module

    3. Insert the power supply in the intended chassis slot, and, using your hand, firmly push on the power supply to ensure proper seating. Do not use excessive force when inserting the power supply.

      An audible click could be heard when the power supply is properly seated and the latch is fully engaged.

      Inserting a Power Supply (Solace PubSub+ 3260 Shown)

    4. Inspect the latch mechanism to ensure that it has engaged the power supply housing. When properly engaged, the latch is fully biased to the right—no portion of the latch slot will be visible to the left of the thumbscrew.

      The figure below shows improperly and properly latched power supplies. The image on the left shows an improperly latched mechanism, which is biased to the left, exposing a large portion of the latch slot. The image on the right shows a properly latched mechanism.

      Improperly and Properly Latched Power Supplies

    5. Firmly hand tighten the latch thumbscrew.
    6. To ensure that the power supply is properly seated and latched, tug on the power supply handle with moderate force. You should not be able to unseat the power supply.
  4. When all of the power supplies are installed in the appliance chassis, insert the female end of a provided AC power cord in each power supply module.
  5. Push firmly on the AC power cord to ensure it is fully inserted in the power supply socket.
  6. For each replaced power supply, insert the male end of each AC power cord in into a three-terminal, single-phase power source that provides AC power within the acceptable range.
  7. Verify that the Green LED located in the upper right-hand corner of each power supply turns on.
  8. Verify that the top LED on the front panel of the appliance is lit blue. When lit, it indicates that the power is on. If you encounter problems, refer to Troubleshooting.

Replacing Solace PubSub+ 3530/3560 Power Supplies

This section describes the procedure for hot swapping an AC power supply in PubSub+ 3530 or PubSub+ 3560. Hot swapping allows you to remove and install a power supply without powering down the appliance.

Use this information in conjunction with the safety information contained herein and the safety guidelines listed in Preparing Your Site.

Step 1: Removing Power Supplies

To remove the failed power supply, follow this procedure:

  1. Use an anti-static wrist strap or other device to ground yourself.
  2. Put on protective gloves to keep from being burned by a hot power supply.
  3. Facing the rear of the appliance, identify the failed power supply by checking the bay that it is installed in, and checking that the Green LED on the power supply is off.

    Power Supply Rear View

  4. Unplug the power cable from the power connector to ensure that power is not supplied.
  5. Grasp the metal handle on the power supply while pushing the release latch to the right (that is, toward the handle), and pull the failed power supply out of the chassis slot.

    Removing a Power Supply from the Chassis

  6. Return the failed power supply to Solace.

Step 2: Installing New Power Supplies

To install a new power supply, follow this procedure:

  1. Allow the power supply to reach room temperature before installing.
  2. Use an anti-static wrist strap or other device to ground yourself.
  3. Inspect the power supply to ensure that the pins and electrical connections are not damaged.
  4. Insert the power supply in the intended chassis slot, and, using your hand, firmly push on the power supply to ensure proper seating. Do not use excessive force when inserting the power supply.

    An audible click is heard when the power supply is properly seated and the latch is fully engaged.

  5. To ensure that the power supply is properly seated and latched, tug on the power supply handle with moderate force. You should not be able to unseat the power supply.
  6. When the power supply is installed in the appliance chassis, insert the female end of a provided AC power cord in the power supply module.
  7. Push firmly on the AC power cord to ensure it is fully inserted in the power supply socket.
  8. Insert the male end of the AC power cord in into a three-terminal, single-phase power source that provides AC power within the acceptable range.
  9. Verify that the Green LED located in the upper right-hand corner of each power supply turns on.
  10. Verify that the LED on the front panel of the appliance is lit blue. When lit, it indicates that the power is on. If you encounter problems, refer to Troubleshooting.

Troubleshooting

If you encounter problems with the hot-swapped power supply installation, check the following items:

  • With the power on, for a PubSub+ 3230/3260 is the blue PWR LED on the front panel on ? For a PubSub+ 3530/3560, is the front LED a steady blue color?
  • If it is unlit, check the AC input, AC source, appliance circuit breaker, and the power supply cable.
  • If the blue power LED is still unlit, the problem might be a power supply failure.
  • If it is flashing, the appliance hardware has successfully initialized but the software has not.
  • Does the appliance shut down after being on a short time?
  • If the system temperatures rise rapidly, the appliance can overheat and shut itself down due to insufficient air flow. Monitor the system temperatures and chassis fan status by entering the show environment User EXEC command.
  • Ensure that the chassis fans are working.
  • Ensure that the chassis intake and exhaust vents are clear.
