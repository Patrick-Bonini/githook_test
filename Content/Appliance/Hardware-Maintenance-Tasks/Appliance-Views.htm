Appliance Front & Rear Panels

This section shows front and rear panels of Solace PubSub+ appliances.

Solace PubSub+ 3230

The front panel of a PubSub+ 3230 (CHS-3230AC-01 and CHS-3230AC-02 chassis) features three LEDs that indicate power status, hard drive status, and ethernet status. Additionally, the front panel features two recessed buttons: the on/standby button and the RST button.

Front Panel View of CHS-3230AC

The rear panel of PubSub+ 3230 features six full size PCIe slots. The rear panel also features two AC power supply bays. A single installed power supply is sufficient in providing power to the appliance; however, the additional power supply is able to provide redundancy, load sharing, and increased event broker availability.

Rear Panel View of CHS-3230AC-02 Chassis

For more details on the CHS-3230AC-01 and CHS-3230AC-02 chassis, refer to Connections, LEDs, and Specifications.

Solace PubSub+ 3260

The front panel of a PubSub+ 3260 features three LEDs that indicate power status, hard drive status, and alarm status. Additionally, the front panel features two recessed buttons: the on/standby button and the RST button.

Front Panel View of Solace CHS-3260AC-02, -03, or -04 Chassis

The figure below shows a rear view of CHS-3260AC-02 chassis. The rear panel features six full size PCIe slots distributed across twin fabric expansion cartridges (FECs). The rear panel also features four AC power supply bays. Two installed power supplies are sufficient in providing power to the appliance; however, the two additional power supplies are able to provide redundancy, load sharing, and increased appliance availability.

Rear Panel View Solace CHS-3260AC-02

For more details on the CHS-3260AC-02 chassis, refer to Connections, LEDs, and Specifications.

The front panel of a CHS‑3530AC chassis features an LED that indicates the power status of the appliance. The front panel also features a hinged portion that provides easy access to the four system drive bays.

Front Panel View of Solace PubSub+ 3530

The rear panel also features an on/standby () button and two AC power supply bays. A single installed power supply is sufficient to provide power to the appliance; however, an additional power supply is able to provide redundancy, load sharing, and increased appliance availability.
