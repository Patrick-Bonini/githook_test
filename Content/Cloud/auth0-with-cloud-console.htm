Auth0 with

You can integrate Auth0 with to enable single sign-on (SSO) and multi-factor authentication (MFA) for your enterprise accounts.

After you have configured Auth0, your organization will receive a customized URL to log in to . If your users have already signed in to their Auth0 account, they will automatically be signed in to . Otherwise, they will be directed to your organization's Auth0 sign-in page to enter their details. Your organization's Auth0 settings determine whether multi-factor authentication (MFA) is used.

If a user belongs to multiple accounts that are associated with your organization's Auth0 ID, they can switch between those accounts without logging in to the accounts separately.

Register as an Application in Auth0

When you set up as an application in Auth0, a Client ID, Client Secret, and Discovery URL is generated that will be used to bind your account to Auth0. These information allow your organization's users to be authenticated by Auth0.

To register as an application in Auth0, do the following:

  1. Click on Applications > Create Application.

  2. Select Regular Web Applications tile, write the application name, and click the Create button.

  3. Select the Settings tab and take note of the following values. You’ll need to provide these values to PubSub+ Cloud team to complete the setup:
    • Client ID
    • Client Secret

  4. Scroll down to the Application URIs section, and enter the URLs provided by PubSub+ Cloud team in the Application Login URI and Allowed Callback URLs fields. Refer to Request a Preferred Subdomain to learn how to get the URL.

  5. Click Save Changes.

  6. Make a note of the following information, which you will need when configuring your SSO in

    • Client ID
    • Client Secret
    • Discovery URL
